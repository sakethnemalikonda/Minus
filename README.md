
# Minus Program

## Overview
The **Minus Program** is a financial discipline model designed to help borrowers reduce loan defaults and help banks lower Non-Performing Assets (NPAs).

The idea encourages borrowers to reduce their loan balance faster through better repayment behavior instead of extending loan tenure.

---

## Problem

Many financial systems face the following problems:

- Borrowers take long-term loans with high interest.
- Customers delay repayments.
- Banks suffer from increasing **Non-Performing Assets (NPAs)**.
- Loan recovery becomes difficult.

---

## Solution: Minus Program

The **Minus Program** encourages borrowers to continuously reduce their loan balance by paying small additional amounts whenever possible.

Instead of stretching the loan period, the borrower moves toward **zero balance (minus)** faster.

This creates a win-win model for **customers, banks, and the economy**.

---

## Benefits for Customers

- Reduce total interest payments
- Close loans faster
- Improve financial discipline
- Increase credit score
- Lower financial stress

---

## Benefits for Banks

- Lower loan defaults
- Reduced Non-Performing Assets (NPAs)
- Higher loan recovery rate
- Improved customer trust

---

## Benefits for the Economy

- Healthier banking system
- Lower financial risk
- More efficient credit flow
- Stronger financial stability

---

## Key Concept

The core idea is simple:

> Encourage borrowers to reduce their outstanding loan balance faster instead of extending loan tenure.

Small additional payments over time significantly reduce interest burden and default risk.
